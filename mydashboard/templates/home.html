PROJECT

Why did we build Eliot?

  • A lot of vaccination must kept between +2°C and +8°C: Measles, rubella, yellow fever, hepatitis B, meningitis, influenza, tetanus, polio, whooping cough, typhoid, chicken pox, etc.
  • Most medicines treating cancer, HIV, hemophilia, multiple sclerosis, leukemia, insulin-dependent diabetes & orphan diseases are also very heat-sensitive.
  • HUG counts +200 heat-sensitive medicines that need to be carefully monitored.

PRODUCT

How does Eliot work?

Eliot is the first pharmaceutical dedicated smart packaging. It allows end-to-end temperature monitoring and lets the user know about the medicine validity.

By default, Eliot displays the drug’s temperature

Eliot allows users to quickly set up maximum & minimum temperature thresholds

Whenever temperature gets too high or too low, Eliot warns you by displaying a message and emitting a sound

Eliot builds a traceability report that the user can regularly check to make sure medicine are still valid

Use Cases

Lisa is a pharmacist. She very regularly deals with heat-sensitive medicines such as cancer, HIV or multiple sclerosis treatments. She knows most transport companies monitor drugs’ temperature but she wants to make absolutely sure she does not sell inefficient products to her beloved customers.

Lola travels a lot. She has diabetes so she regularly takes medicine. When she is in hot countries, she is sometimes afraid her drugs are no longer effective because of the heat. She would like to have a device allowing her to track her medicine temperature.

Aleksander needs to do the tetanus vaccination. He bought the product at the drugstore and will bring it to the doctor next week for the injection. He does not know about heat-sensitive medicine and leaves the tetanus vaccination on his desk, in the 25°C environement for 5 days. He has not idea the medicine is probably no longer efficient.
